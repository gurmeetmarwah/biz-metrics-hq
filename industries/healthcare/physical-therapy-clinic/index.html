Operator benchmark sources

  • BizMetricsHQ owner-operator panel — Survey data from U.S. outpatient physical therapy clinics ($500K–$1.8M revenue), normalized for solo vs multi-therapist models.
  • Business-for-sale listings (BizBuySell, PT practice brokers) — Physical therapy clinic listings with disclosed revenue, EBITDA, SDE, and therapist count (2023–2026).
  • APTA Private Practice Section benchmarks — National PT practice revenue, visit volume, reimbursement, and staffing trends.
  • SBA 7(a) loan performance (NAICS 621340) — Loan performance benchmarks for PT clinic acquisitions and startups.
  • CMS Medicare Physician Fee Schedule (PT services) — Reimbursement benchmarks for evaluation and treatment codes.

Calculation formulas

  • EBITDA margin

    (Revenue − Clinical Payroll − Admin Payroll − Rent − Marketing − Overhead) ÷ Revenue × 100

    Median ~23%; healthy range 18–28% for outpatient PT clinics.

  • Revenue per therapist

    Annual Revenue ÷ Number of Full-Time Physical Therapists

    Median ~$420K per PT for general outpatient practices.

  • Revenue per visit

    Total Collected Revenue ÷ Total Billable Visits

    Typical $90–$140 per visit depending on payer mix and service level.

  • Patient lifetime value

    Revenue Per Episode × Average Episodes Per Patient × Retention Years

    Typical active patient generates $900–$2,400 over 3–5 years.

  • SDE valuation

    SDE × Industry Multiple (median 3.2×)

    SDE = Net Profit + Owner PT Compensation + add-backs. Range 2.5×–4.0× SDE.

Limitations

  • Benchmarks reflect U.S. outpatient physical therapy clinics; home health and hospital-based PT vary significantly.
  • Payer mix (Medicare, commercial, workers' comp, cash-pay) materially affects revenue per visit and margins.
  • Direct-access vs referral-dependent clinics differ on marketing spend and new patient acquisition costs.
  • Figures are directional planning tools — not substitutes for CPA review, practice valuation, or legal advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

How profitable are physical therapy clinics?

Well-run outpatient PT clinics achieve 18–28% EBITDA margins, with a median around 23%. Net profit after owner therapist compensation typically runs 12–20%. Clinical payroll (28–36% of revenue) and rent (7–11%) are the largest controllable costs.

How much revenue does a PT clinic generate?

Outpatient physical therapy clinics typically generate $500K–$1.5M annually, with a median around $850K. Solo practices run $400K–$650K; multi-therapist clinics push $950K–$1.5M+. Sports and orthopedic specialty clinics can exceed $1.2M.

How much do PT clinic owners earn?

Solo PT owners typically earn $100K–$180K in total compensation, with a median around $165K. Multi-therapist and multi-location owners can earn $140K–$500K+. Associate physical therapists earn $75K–$110K before partnership or buy-in.

What is a PT clinic worth?

Independent PT clinics sell for 2.5×–4.0× SDE, with a median of 3.2×. A clinic with $850K revenue and $240K SDE might be valued around $770K. Strong referral networks, diversified payer mix, and high therapist utilization command the high end.

What is a good EBITDA margin?

A healthy PT clinic EBITDA margin is 20–26%. Below 18% signals overhead bloat or low revenue per therapist. Top-quartile clinics hit 28%+ through efficient scheduling, strong referral relationships, and optimized therapist-to-support-staff ratios.

How many patients should a therapist see?

A productive outpatient PT typically handles 12–18 visits per day at 72–88% utilization. Clinics maintain 800–2,000 active patients with 2–6 full-time therapists. New patient flow of 25–55 per month supports growth.
