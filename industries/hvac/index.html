Frequently Asked Questions

How profitable is an HVAC business?

Healthy HVAC companies typically achieve 8–16% net profit margin, with a median around 12%. Top-quartile operators with strong maintenance contract penetration and disciplined labor management can reach 17–22%. Gross margins usually run 45–55% before overhead.

What is average HVAC revenue?

The median owner-operated HVAC company generates about $2.4M in annual revenue. The interquartile range spans $1.2M (25th percentile) to $3.2M (75th percentile), with top-performing companies exceeding $5M. Revenue varies significantly based on technician count, service area, and maintenance contract mix.

How much do HVAC owners earn?

HVAC business owners typically earn $95K–$185K in total compensation, with a median around $145K. Small single-truck operators often earn $95K–$120K, while multi-location operators can exceed $350K when combining owner salary and profit distributions.

What is an HVAC company worth?

HVAC businesses typically sell at 2.4×–3.8× SDE, with a median near 3.1×. A company with $2.4M revenue and $400K SDE might value between $960K and $1.52M. Companies with high recurring maintenance revenue and transferable management often command premium multiples.

Are HVAC businesses recession resistant?

HVAC is moderately recession-resistant. Emergency repairs and essential heating/cooling needs persist in downturns, and maintenance contracts provide recurring revenue stability. However, discretionary installations and commercial new construction can slow. Companies with 35%+ maintenance revenue tend to weather cycles better.

Operator benchmark sources

  • BizMetricsHQ owner-operator panel — Survey data from U.S. HVAC companies ($800K–$6M revenue), normalized for residential vs commercial mix and maintenance contract penetration.
  • Business-for-sale listings (BizBuySell, HVAC business brokers) — HVAC company listings with disclosed revenue, SDE, and technician count (2023–2026).
  • ACCA — HVAC industry financial benchmarks — National HVAC contractor revenue, expense ratios, and labor productivity trends.
  • SBA 7(a) loan performance (NAICS 238220) — Loan performance benchmarks for HVAC business acquisitions and startups.
  • IRS Statistics of Income — Schedule C (NAICS 238220) — Sole-proprietorship revenue and expense ratios for HVAC contractors.

Calculation formulas

  • Net profit margin

    (Revenue − Payroll − Materials − Fleet − Marketing − Overhead) ÷ Revenue × 100

    Median ~12%; healthy range 8–16% for owner-operated HVAC companies.

  • Revenue per technician

    Annual Revenue ÷ Number of Full-Time Field Technicians

    Median ~$240K per technician for mixed service/install companies.

  • SDE valuation

    SDE × Industry Multiple (median 3.1×)

    SDE = Net Profit + Owner Compensation + add-backs. Range 2.4×–3.8× SDE.

  • Maintenance contract penetration

    Maintenance Revenue ÷ Total Revenue × 100

    Target 35%+ for recession-resilient revenue mix.

Limitations

  • Benchmarks reflect U.S. owner-operated HVAC contractors; large mechanical contractors and union shops vary significantly.
  • Residential-heavy vs commercial-heavy companies have materially different margin and revenue-per-employee profiles.
  • Seasonal markets (Northern heating, Southern cooling) affect monthly cash flow patterns not captured in annual figures.
  • Figures are directional planning tools — not substitutes for CPA review, business valuation, or legal advice.
